Phase 1D — Focal Length Recovery

Given N 3-D points (from MASt3R depth estimation) and their 2-D image projections, recover the camera focal length f and a depth shift δ via closed-form least-squares. Model: u = f·(X/Z) + cx, v = f·(Y/Z) + cy. The optimal f = Σ(A·b) / Σ(A²) where A=[X/Z, Y/Z], b=[u−cx, v−cy]. The depth shift δ is found by 3 Newton steps minimising reprojection error.

Phase 1F — Contact Detection

For each candidate foot/hand vertex: (1) project to image using camera intrinsics, (2) sample the depth map at the projected pixel, (3) if |vertex_Z − depth_Z| < threshold (default 5 cm) → contact. Green vertices are in contact with the floor; red vertices are lifted. Switch between "standing" (both feet down) and "walking" (one foot lifted).
